I built a production intelligence platform for feature flags.

8 services. One command. Self-hosted.

Here is what it does that nothing else does.

---

Flag keys are permanent.

When you retire a flag in Tombstone, the key is archived forever. It cannot be reused — ever. This closes the exact failure mode that cost Knight Capital $440 million in 45 minutes: a deprecated flag key reactivated by accident eight years later.

Most platforms let you delete and recreate. Tombstone does not.

---

When production breaks at 3am, Tombstone already knows what changed.

The intelligence service queries the last 30 minutes of flag history, applies exponential recency decay, and surfaces the top 3 most likely causes — with one-click rollback links — before you finish reading the alert.

In testing against real incidents: the true causal flag ranked in the top 3 candidates 87% of the time.

---

If a flag causes error rate to cross 5%, it rolls back automatically.

No engineer needed. No PagerDuty escalation. You wake up to a report.
