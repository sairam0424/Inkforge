A few weeks ago I posted about how feature flags fail at scale.

Knight Capital. $440M. 45 minutes. One flag key that was never properly archived.

I wasn't writing about that as a cautionary tale.

I was writing about it because I had lived a version of it.

---

I was once on call at 2am, staring at a dashboard full of red, and the first thing that came to mind wasn't "What broke?" — it was "Which flags are on right now?"

I did not have a good answer. That was the problem.

200+ flags across 12 services. No consolidated view. We dug through Slack, opened Confluence, made guesses. Eventually found the root cause — a flag key deleted and recreated six months later by a different team, old Redis-cached targeting rules never invalidated.

47 minutes of investigation. Should have been a 5-minute rollback.

---

That post described the problem. This is what I built to solve it.

Tombstone is a self-hosted production intelligence platform for feature flags. Three things it does that nothing else does:

Flag keys are permanently archived when retired. Cannot be reused — ever. The Knight Capital failure mode, closed by design at the database level.

When production breaks, it automatically correlates the last 30 minutes of flag changes, ranks them by recency decay, and surfaces the top 3 suspects with one-click rollback links. The suspects are ready before you finish reading the alert.

If a flag causes error rate to cross 5%, it rolls back automatically. No engineer needed. You wake up to a report, not an escalation.
